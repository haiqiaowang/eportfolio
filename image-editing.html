Image Editing

This page showcases my bitmap and vector imagery projects, created using GIMP and Inkscape. The work explores how visual design can communicate ideas about learning, creativity, and music education.

Book Cover: Learning to See, Learning to Hear

Design Description

This book cover visualizes learning as both perception and expression, inspired by Elliot Eisner’s The Arts and the Creation of Mind. The black-and-white photograph of a man in an art gallery represents reflection, careful observation, and intellectual engagement—the act of “learning to see.” In contrast, the warm-toned piano keys convey creativity, emotion, and the sensory depth of “learning to hear.”

Tools & Techniques

Using GIMP, I combined two photographs through layered editing and gradient blending to create a smooth transition between the visual and auditory symbolism. The minimalist typography emphasizes clarity and balance, echoing Eisner’s belief that the arts cultivate nuanced ways of knowing and understanding the world.

Logo Design: Infinite Keys Studio

Design Description

The logo for Infinite Keys Studio symbolizes the endless potential of learning through piano education. The infinity symbol communicates continuous growth, while the left loop incorporates piano keys to connect structure with creativity. A warm terracotta color suggests passion and artistry, and the muted sage green reflects balance and thoughtful reflection.

Tools & Techniques

The logo was designed in Inkscape using geometric shapes and path operations. I began with two overlapping circles, converting them into paths to construct the infinity symbol. Inside the left loop, a terracotta semicircle holds simplified piano keys made from narrow rectangles in alternating tones. The sage green and terracotta palette reinforces a calm yet creative atmosphere. Finally, the text “INFINITE KEYS STUDIO” was set in a modern sans-serif typeface and centered to create a clean, minimalist composition.

Reflection

Through this image editing work, I deepened my understanding of how bitmap and vector tools support different kinds of visual problem-solving. GIMP allowed me to experiment with photographic composition, blending, and mood, while Inkscape required precise control of shapes, curves, and typography. Together, these projects helped me think more intentionally about how visual design can represent educational philosophies and invite viewers into an imaginative learning space.
